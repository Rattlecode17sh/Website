About Movie

Iron Man, Thor, the Hulk and the rest of the Avengers unite to battle their most powerful enemy yet -- the evil Thanos. On a mission to collect all six Infinity Stones, Thanos plans to use the artifacts to inflict his twisted will on reality. The fate of the planet and existence itself has never been more uncertain as everything the Avengers have fought for has led up to this moment.

Avengers: Infinity War is a 2018 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics superhero team the Avengers, produced by Marvel Studios and distributed by Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. It is the nineteenth film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). It is the sequel to 2012's The Avengers and 2015's Avengers: Age of Ultron.

As of May 2, 2018, Avengers: Infinity War has grossed $322.8 million in the United States and Canada, and $534.7 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $857.5 million.[6] Its worldwide opening weekend of $640.5 million, is the biggest of all time, beating The Fate of the Furious' $541.9 million Avengers: Infinity War was nominated for three MTV Movie & TV Awards including Best Movie, Best Villain (Josh Brolin), and Best Fight (Scarlett Johansson, Danai Gurira, Elizabeth Olsen vs. Carrie Coon)..
